What Is Marketing ROI?

Marketing ROI (Return on Investment) measures how much revenue you generate for every dollar you invest in marketing. It's the clearest measure of whether your marketing spend is paying off — and it applies across every channel from Google Ads to content marketing to email.

Formula: Marketing ROI = ((Revenue from Marketing − Marketing Cost) ÷ Marketing Cost) × 100

A Marketing ROI of 200% means you earned $3 in revenue for every $1 you spent — your cost back, plus twice as much again. For Australian SMBs, a Marketing ROI of 100–200% is considered average across most channels. Above 200% is strong performance.

A negative ROI means your campaign cost more than it generated. An ROI between 0% and 100% is technically profitable, but below what most AU businesses achieve when campaigns are properly optimised.

The most common causes of low marketing ROI are poor audience targeting, weak creative or copy, and a landing page that isn't converting. Fixing any one of these typically lifts ROI significantly — you don't need to overhaul your entire strategy to see results.
